FHA LOAN UNDERWRITING GUIDELINES
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Source: HUD Handbook 4000.1

CREDIT SCORE REQUIREMENTS
- Minimum credit score of 580: eligible for 3.5% down payment
- Credit score 500-579: eligible for 10% down payment only
- Credit score below 500: NOT eligible for FHA financing

DEBT-TO-INCOME (DTI) REQUIREMENTS
- Front-end DTI (housing expenses only): maximum 31%
- Back-end DTI (all debts): maximum 43%
- Exception: DTI up to 57% may be allowed with compensating factors:
  * Verified cash reserves (3+ months PITI)
  * Minimal payment shock (<5% increase in housing payment)
  * Residual income meeting VA guidelines

DOWN PAYMENT REQUIREMENTS
- Minimum 3.5% for credit scores >= 580
- Minimum 10% for credit scores 500-579
- Down payment sources: personal savings, gift funds, grants

MORTGAGE INSURANCE PREMIUM (MIP)
- Upfront MIP: 1.75% of base loan amount
- Annual MIP: 0.55% for loans > 15 years with LTV > 90%
- MIP duration: Life of loan if down payment < 10%

PROPERTY ELIGIBILITY
- Must be borrower's primary residence
- Must meet FHA minimum property standards (MPS)
- Property types: SFR, 2-4 units, condos (FHA-approved), manufactured homes
- Maximum loan limits vary by county (2024 Austin TX: $531,250)

EMPLOYMENT AND INCOME
- Minimum 2-year employment history required
- Self-employed: 2 years federal tax returns required
- Gap in employment > 6 months: must document and explain
- Commission income: 2-year average used for qualifying

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION
1. Completed Uniform Residential Loan Application (Form 1003)
2. W-2 statements for the past 2 years
3. Federal income tax returns for the past 2 years
4. Pay stubs covering most recent 30-day period
5. Bank statements for most recent 2 months (all accounts)
6. Gift letter (if using gift funds for down payment)
7. Copy of signed purchase contract
8. Photo ID

APPRAISAL REQUIREMENTS
- FHA-approved appraiser required
- Must meet HUD Minimum Property Requirements
- Appraised value must support purchase price
- If repairs required: must be completed before closing OR escrow holdback
